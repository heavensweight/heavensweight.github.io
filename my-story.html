What They Preached Was Peace, What I Lived Was Pain

Growing up in a strictly religious household, I was taught that faith was the foundation of everything. The constant pressure of being perfect in the eyes of my community and family made my childhood feel suffocating. My every move, word, and action was scrutinized through the lens of religious doctrine. What was supposed to be peace, turned into a life filled with guilt, fear, and deep emotional pain.

For years, I struggled to meet the standards set for me, never feeling good enough. Each time I failed, the weight of disappointment from my family felt unbearable. I wasn’t just failing in their eyes, but in the eyes of my religion, my faith, and my very identity.

But even as I was surrounded by the loud voices preaching peace, I couldn't ignore the pain I was living through. I began to question everything: the teachings, the rituals, and even the people I loved most. Why was I supposed to follow a path that didn't feel right for me? What if there was more to life than just living in fear?

Over time, I began to unlearn the beliefs that had been ingrained in me. Reclaiming my right to think freely, to explore life on my own terms, was a hard but necessary journey. The pain of questioning everything I had once believed was immense, but the freedom it brought was worth it. I was no longer a prisoner of my past. I was finally free.

It wasn’t easy, and I still struggle at times. But today, I live my life with purpose and clarity, knowing that I have the power to make my own choices. I hope my story can inspire others to reclaim their freedom too—freedom from fear, guilt, and the burden of living for someone else’s ideals.

Thank you for reading.
